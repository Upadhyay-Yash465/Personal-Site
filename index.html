KnightRaas · UCF Competitive Garba-Raas · Captain Intern

Garba and Raas are classical folk forms from Gujarat — circle dances rooted in devotion, refined over centuries, now run through a competitive circuit where teams are judged on synchronization, footwork, formation, and cultural authenticity. The season runs through winter. Nationals is the final tournament.

I'm Captain Intern of KnightRaas, which means I sit at the junction between the floor and leadership: choreography input, formation calls, member accountability, comp-day floor management. The team just won nationals. That outcome doesn't happen by accident — it's the result of a season of that kind of work.

Garba-Raas is also the most culturally specific thing I do. South Asian diaspora expression in a competitive frame, with real weight behind it. I don't take that lightly.

Process

Research first. Build a structure. Execute. I don't separate personal from professional from creative — it's all one project. I'd rather be asked a hard question than handed a conclusion. I'm suspicious of anything that needs to be polished before it can be true.

Writing something down weakens your ownership of it — so I'm selective about what I commit to paper.

Worldbuilding

Two original universes in active development. Not portfolio pieces — open windows into how I think creatively.

Lucky Boy

ink · identity · insight · cost

Virus bugs consume insight. The enriched ink gets tattooed onto fighters — each person wields their specific interpretation of a concept, not the concept itself. Your understanding of speed is not mine. That difference is the power system.

The logic: what you know can be taken. What's written into skin cannot.

Tower

sacrifice · body · ritual · environment

Mythical beast body parts are sacrificed in ritual to strengthen body or environment. Different parts, different yields. Power costs a body — and the body is never yours alone.

The logic: you don't wield power. You trade for it.

Both share a trinity: ritual, body as site of transformation, identity cost. Recurring themes across both — identity under pressure, the cost of becoming something, hollowness.
